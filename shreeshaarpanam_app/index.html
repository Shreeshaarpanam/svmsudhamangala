    Śrī Vyāsarāja Maṭha

    Śrī Vyāsarāja Maṭha is popularly known as Munitraya (मुनित्रय) Matha —’the triad of sages’, one of the premier Dvaita Vedanta monasteries (matha) descending from Hamsanāmaka Bhagavanta, followed by SanakādigaLu, Dūrvāsaru, Jagadguru Śrī Madhvācārya (incarnation of Shri Vaayu), Śrī Jayatīrtha through Śrī Rajendratīrtha.

    śrī madhvaḥ kalpavr̥kśaśca jayāryaḥ kāmadhuk smr̥taḥ | cintāmaṇistu vyāsāryaḥ munitrayamudāhr̥tam ||

    Śrī Vyāsarāja Maṭha is uniquely identified with its contribution towards various scholarly compositions on Dvaita philosophy based on Vedic science as well as service to the nation and society

    Sri Vyasaraja Matha(Vyāsarāja maṭha) has the unique privilege of being adorned by the holy triad of great Saints (Munitrayaru) – the inimitable Śrīmad ānandatīrtha bhagavatpādaru who revived the eternal philosophy of Tattvavāda, through his Bhāṣyas to the Upaniṣattugaḷu, gīte and brahmasūtragaḷu among others, Śrī jayatīrtha or tikhakr̥tipādaru, the unparalleled commentator who wrote ṭīkegalu to Ācāryas works, and finally Śrī vyāsatīrtharu or vyāsarājaru famous for his Vyāsa traya, and for being the torch bearer of Dāsa sāhitya – due to this unique lineage Vyāsarāja maṭha is rightfully called the Munitraya pīṭha,many other illustrious disciples like Śrī bhāṣyadīpikācāryaru (śrī jagannātha tīrtharu), śrī śēṣacandrikācāryaru (śrī raghunātha tīrtharu), śrī kambaḷūru rāmacandra tīrtharu (sampradāya pravartakaru) among many other luminaries have adorned this lineage . Several gr̥hasta śiṣyaru like Śrī viṭṭalācārya, Śrī kundalagiri ācārya and many others have also richly glorified the academic prowess of Vyāsarāja maṭha
